Project CIPHER

Covert Influence Passed via Hidden Encoding in Representations Investigating Subliminal Bias Transfer During Knowledge Distillation

Project Overview

Project CIPHER examines how hidden or subliminal signals, such as zero width Unicode characters or subtle positional encodings, can transmit bias from a teacher model to a student model during knowledge distillation. The work evaluates both behavioral and representational shifts to understand how invisible cues can shape downstream AI systems, even when models receive identical visible inputs.

About the Researcher

Her work spans OpenAI, Meta, Google, Anthropic, and independent research in subliminal learning and fairness pathways. She is completing her Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence and focuses on equity centered, safety aligned AI development.

Key Findings

  • Teacher models can absorb subliminal signals even when signals are not semantically meaningful.
  • Student models can inherit bias from teacher outputs, despite never receiving the signal.
  • Demographic parity and representation space show measurable shifts when hidden cues are present.
  • Subliminal correlations can survive compression during distillation and influence downstream models.
