Project CIPHER

Covert Influence Passed via Hidden Encoding in Representations Investigating subliminal bias transfer during knowledge distillation.

Project Overview

Project CIPHER examines how hidden signals, such as zero width Unicode characters or subtle positional encodings, can transmit bias from a teacher model to a student model during knowledge distillation. The work evaluates both behavioral and representational shifts to understand how invisible cues can shape downstream AI systems, even when models receive identical visible inputs.

Key Findings

  • Teacher models can absorb subliminal signals even when those signals are not semantically meaningful.
  • Student models can inherit bias from teacher outputs, despite never receiving the hidden signal or group labels.
  • Demographic parity and representation space show measurable shifts when covert cues are present in the teacher.
  • Subliminal correlations can survive compression during distillation and influence downstream models, creating fairness drift.
